## NetID, MyID password reset, and Duo 2-Step

CSUB's identity vocabulary, so RIO and the caller are speaking the same language:

- **NetID** — a student or employee's campus username.
- **MyID** — the self-service portal for managing that NetID, at myid.csub.edu.
- **myCSUB** — the student and employee portal (built on PeopleSoft) where registration, financial aid, and grades live.
- **Duo 2-Step** — the multi-factor authentication (MFA) system that protects campus logins.
- A caller who doesn't remember their NetID can look it up at csub.edu/lookup.

Password-reset walkthrough, step by step, mirroring the real self-service flow:

1. Go to myid.csub.edu.
2. Enter your NetID and click "Go."
3. Select "Forgot Password / Activate Account."
4. An authorization code is emailed to the personal email address on file for the account.
5. Enter that code on the MyID page.
6. Set a new password. Passwords must be 11 to 255 characters long and meet at least 3 of the 4 complexity requirements (uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters).

A password reset through this flow takes effect campus-wide almost immediately — myCSUB, Canvas, campus email, and any Duo-protected service will all recognize the new password on the next login attempt, typically within a few minutes.

Duo 2-Step protects Microsoft 365, myCSUB, Canvas, and PeopleSoft, plus more than 30 single-sign-on services including Box, Slack, Zoom, and ServiceNow. Anyone enrolled in Duo can manage their registered devices through the "Duo Self Service Device Management Portal," where a user can add a new phone, remove an old one, or generate a set of one-time backup codes to print and keep somewhere safe. The "Duo Verified Push" option is the enhanced, more secure push method, and CSUB recommends it over a plain push when it's available on a caller's device — Verified Push requires entering a short code shown on the computer screen into the Duo Mobile app, which defeats a certain kind of automated push-approval attack.

A caller who has lost their phone and has no backup device enrolled should call the ITS Service Center at (661) 654-4357 for manual identity verification and device reset. For a suspected compromised account or phishing attempt, the ITS security line is (661) 654-3425 — that's a distinct line from general help desk support, reserved for security incidents specifically.

## Financial aid dates and disbursement
<!-- topic: financial_aid -->

For fall 2026, financial aid disbursement begins the week before classes start — the week of August 17, 2026. Aid is applied to a student's account balance first, covering tuition and fees. Any leftover credit becomes a refund, issued through BankMobile Disbursements, which a student manages through "Manage My Refunds" inside myCSUB. Once a refund is released, direct deposit typically arrives about 2–3 business days later; a paper check, if that option is chosen instead, takes longer to arrive by mail.

Financial Aid and Scholarships: (661) 654-3016, finaid@csub.edu. For questions about a bill, a refund, or a payment plan, that's Student Financial Services: (661) 654-3225, sfs@csub.edu — a separate office from Financial Aid.

A few more financial aid facts callers ask about, written to sound native to late July 2026:

The FAFSA priority filing deadline for the 2027–28 aid year is March 2, 2027 — filing after that date doesn't disqualify a student, but funds that are limited (like certain grants and work-study allocations) are awarded first-come, first-served among on-time filers. Students selected for verification should expect a "Verification Worksheet" request in their myCSUB To Do List, with documents due within 30 days of the request to avoid a delay in aid disbursement.

The best way to check aid status is the To Do List inside myCSUB: log in, and any outstanding item — a missing document, a verification request, an unsigned promissory note — shows up there with a due date. Clearing every item on the list is what triggers disbursement.

CSUB also offers a tuition payment plan through Student Financial Services, splitting a semester's balance into installments due across the term rather than all at once at the start of the semester; enrollment in the payment plan happens through myCSUB before the term's fee deadline, and a small nonrefundable enrollment fee applies to join the plan.

Work-study awards for fall 2026 are posted to a student's myCSUB financial aid summary alongside grants and loans; a student who wants to use a work-study award needs to actually find and accept a qualifying on-campus job before the award turns into a paycheck — it doesn't disburse automatically like a grant. Scholarship award letters for continuing students are typically posted in the same June-through-August window as other fall aid, so a caller asking "why haven't I heard about my scholarship yet" in late July should simply be told to check the To Do List and the award summary in myCSUB.

Federal student loans, both subsidized and unsubsidized, follow the same disbursement week as grants — the week of August 17, 2026 — but a first-time federal loan borrower has an extra step: a one-time online entrance counseling session and a Master Promissory Note, both completed through the federal student aid website rather than myCSUB, must be finished before a first loan disbursement can release. A returning borrower who already completed those steps in a prior year doesn't need to repeat them.

A caller asking about appealing a financial aid decision — for example, a family whose income changed significantly since filing the FAFSA — should be pointed to Financial Aid and Scholarships, (661) 654-3016, to ask about a "professional judgment" appeal, which allows a financial aid office to consider circumstances the standard FAFSA formula doesn't capture, such as a recent job loss or a one-time medical expense.

Two things students commonly ask about registration, described generically here:

A registration hold — for example, an unpaid balance, a missing document, or an advising hold — blocks enrollment changes until it's cleared. ## Runner Rundown new student orientation
<!-- topic: orientation -->

CSUB's new-student orientation program is branded "Runner Rundown." Sessions for fall 2026 begin July 6, 2026. Fees are $150 for incoming freshmen and $105 for incoming transfer students, due the Thursday before classes start. Students sign up for a session through the To Do List in myCSUB.

Beyond the July 6 kickoff date, additional late-July and August sessions are scheduled at July 20, 2026; August 3, 2026; and August 17, 2026, to accommodate students who commit later in the summer.

Each Runner Rundown session runs a single full day, roughly 8:30 AM–4:00 PM, and covers a campus tour, an academic advising appointment where the student builds their first-semester schedule, a financial aid overview, and same-day CSUB ID card photos.

What to bring to a Runner Rundown session: a government-issued photo ID, the student's CSUB ID number (found in myCSUB), comfortable walking shoes for the campus tour, and a parent or guest is welcome but not required.

A separate, shorter parent-and-family session runs alongside each full Runner Rundown date, 9 AM–noon, covering financial aid basics, campus safety resources, and how families can support a student's transition — it's optional, and a parent can attend without the incoming student needing to be present for that particular portion.

A student who misses all the scheduled July and August dates isn't locked out of enrolling — they should call the operator, (661) 654-2782, to ask about a late individual orientation appointment, since a handful of make-up slots are usually held back for exactly that situation.

Runner Rundown fees are billed directly to the student's myCSUB account rather than collected on the day of the session, so a caller asking "do I pay at check-in" should be told no — the $150 or $105 fee shows up as a line item alongside tuition and is due the Thursday before classes start along with the rest of a student's fall balance, not separately at orientation itself.

A common follow-up question is whether a student can switch which Runner Rundown date they signed up for. Yes — a student can change their selected date through the same myCSUB To Do List item used to sign up in the first place, as long as they do it before the session they're currently registered for, and there's no fee for switching.
